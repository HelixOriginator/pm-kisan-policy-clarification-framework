About the Scheme

What is PM-KISAN?

Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi — a Government of India income support scheme for eligible landholding farmer families.

Empowering India's Farming Community

PM-KISAN provides financial support directly to eligible farmer families across India. The scheme operates based on state-verified landholding records submitted to the central government, ensuring transparent and systematic disbursement.

💵

₹6,000 Annual Support

Direct income support provided per year to eligible beneficiary families.

🏦

Direct Benefit Transfer

Funds credited directly to verified bank accounts linked with Aadhaar.

📋

State-Verified Records

Beneficiaries identified through state land records and verification processes.

🇮🇳

Pan-India Coverage

Implemented by the Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare nationwide.

Key Scheme Highlights

  • ✓ ₹6,000 per year to eligible beneficiaries
  • ✓ Paid in three equal installments of ₹2,000
  • ✓ Transferred via Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT)
  • ✓ Aadhaar linkage generally required
  • ✓ State-verified landholding records used
  • ✓ Payment status verifiable on official portal
  • ✓ Eligibility subject to official updates

Eligibility Criteria

Who Qualifies for PM-KISAN?

Based on official operational guidelines — eligibility is subject to official updates and periodic revisions.

✅
Eligible Beneficiaries
Generally include the following
  • ✓ Landholding farmer families as per official records
  • ✓ Beneficiaries identified through state land records
  • ✓ Verified data submitted by state governments to the centre
  • ✓ Farmers with valid Aadhaar and bank account linkage
✕
Exclusion Categories
Typically excluded from the scheme
  • ✕ Institutional landholders
  • ✕ Holders of constitutional posts
  • ✕ Income tax payers (as per applicable criteria)
  • ✕ Serving or retired government officers above specified levels
  • ✕ Professionals — doctors, engineers, lawyers (as defined in guidelines)

Common Concerns

Concerns & Policy Clarifications

Addressing frequently raised concerns with factual policy clarifications and context.

01
"PM-KISAN excludes small or marginal farmers."
▼
📋 Policy Clarification

Eligibility is based on recorded landholding ownership rather than land size category, subject to applicable guidelines and revisions.

💡 Where Confusion May Arise

Land record discrepancies, inheritance disputes, or digitization delays can create the impression of exclusion when the root cause is administrative.

02
"Funds do not reach real beneficiaries."
▼
📋 Policy Clarification

Payments are processed through Direct Benefit Transfer to verified bank accounts linked with beneficiary records.

💡 Documented Challenges

Aadhaar mismatches, banking errors, incorrect account details, or delays in state-level data validation can cause payment failures or delays.

03
"Beneficiary selection lacks transparency."
▼
📋 Policy Clarification

States are responsible for identifying eligible beneficiaries based on land records and submitting verified lists to the central government.

💡 Ground Reality

Data accuracy depends on local land record systems and timely updates. Variations in administrative capacity across states affect the quality of beneficiary lists.

Implementation Challenges

Documented Challenges

Publicly reported implementation issues that can vary by state and administrative capacity.

🗺️

Incomplete Land Record Digitization

Many states still have partially digitized land records, creating gaps in beneficiary identification.

⌨️

Data Entry Errors

Manual data entry processes can introduce errors in names, account numbers, and other critical fields.

🔗

Aadhaar-Bank Linkage Issues

Mismatches between Aadhaar details and bank records can cause payment failures or redirections.

⏳

Delays in State Verification

Varying state-level processing timelines affect the speed at which eligible farmers receive payments.

👥

Beneficiary Duplication

Detection and removal of duplicate entries remains an ongoing data quality challenge.

📊

Administrative Capacity

Implementation quality varies based on local governance infrastructure and technical capabilities.
